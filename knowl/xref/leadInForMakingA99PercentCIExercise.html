Guided Practice 5.2.7.

If \(X\) is a normally distributed random variable, how often will \(X\) be within 2.58 standard deviations of the mean?
 4 
This is equivalent to asking how often the Z-score will be larger than -2.58 but less than 2.58. (For a picture, see Figure 5.2.8.) There is \(\approx 0.99\) probability that the unobserved random variable \(X\) will be within 2.58 standard deviations of the mean.
