Checkpoint 3.2.26.

Let \(X\) and \(Y\) represent the outcomes of rolling two dice. (a) What is the probability that the first die, \(X\text{,}\) is 1? (b) What is the probability that both \(X\) and \(Y\) are 1? (c) Use the formula for conditional probability to compute \(P(Y =\) 1 \(| X =\) 1\()\text{.}\) (d) What is \(P(Y=1)\text{?}\) Is this different from the answer from part (c)? Explain.
 15 
Brief solutions: (a) \(1/6\text{.}\) (b) \(1/36\text{.}\) (c) \(\frac{P(Y=1 \text{ and } X=1)}{P(X=1)} = \frac{1/36}{1/6}=1/6\text{.}\) (d) The probability is the same as in part (c): \(P(Y = 1) = 1/6\text{.}\) The probability that \(Y = 1\) was unchanged by knowledge about \(X\text{,}\) which makes sense as \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent.
